Veteran Career Clarity Assessment

Who This Is For

This assessment was built for veterans who are 2 to 10 years out of service. People who have navigated the transition, built something functional in civilian life, and are carrying a persistent sense that something still isn't right. The external picture looks fine. The internal experience is something else.

If that sounds familiar, this report was built for you. It is designed to name what you have been carrying and give that clarity somewhere specific to go.

What This Assessment Measures

The Veteran Career Clarity Assessment evaluates three pillars of post-service alignment. These pillars form a connected system. Your sense of identity shapes how you approach work. Your relationship to your work affects how you show up in your relationships. How you navigate your relationships reflects and reinforces how you see yourself. When one is off, the others feel it.

Pillar 1 — Identity & Purpose

Measures how clearly you have built a sense of self that exists independently of your military service and current job title. A strong score here means your identity is grounded in who you are, not what you did or what you do.

Pillar 2 — Career & Belonging

Measures whether your work reflects genuine contribution, your real capabilities, and your values — not just what made practical sense after separation. A strong score here means your professional life is aligned with who you actually are.

Pillar 3 — Relationships & Reintegration

Measures the quality of your civilian connections, your emotional presence, and your capacity to ask for support when you need it. A strong score here means you have built genuine community and are not carrying everything alone.

How the Scoring Works

Each question was answered on a five-point scale from Never to Almost Always. Some questions were scored in reverse, meaning a lower response on a negatively framed question reflects higher alignment. All scoring was handled before your results were calculated, so the numbers in your report reflect your actual alignment level in each area. Your pillar scores are expressed as both a raw average and a percentage, and each falls into one of three bands:

Aligned

Strong alignment in this pillar. The foundation is solid. The work here is to sustain what is working and identify where the next level of clarity lives.

Emerging

Partial alignment with meaningful gaps. The awareness is present, but something between awareness and full integration is not yet consistent. The most common band and the most workable starting point.

Disconnected

Significant alignment gaps in this pillar. The friction here is real and is likely creating drag in the other areas as well. This band calls for direct attention.

How to Read Your Report

Your report is organized into three pillar sections followed by a cross-pillar analysis and a priority focus recommendation. Read the pillar sections first to understand each system individually, then read the full picture to understand how they are interacting. The priority focus tells you where to put your energy first and gives you a specific action to take within the next seven days.

This report is a diagnostic tool. It surfaces patterns, names what is present, and identifies the most useful starting point for the work. It is the beginning of a more specific conversation.

What Comes Next

Reframe & Rise works specifically with veterans in the years-out, looks-fine phase of post-service life. The coaching work that follows this report is built around the RISE Method: Reflect, Integrate, Strengthen, Execute. It is direct, practical, and grounded in the specific experience of building a civilian life that is genuinely yours.

If anything in your report named something worth exploring further, the next step is a straightforward conversation. No commitment required. You can reach out at reframerise.com when it feels right.
